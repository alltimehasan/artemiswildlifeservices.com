Hogs

Our Method to Remove Hogs

1. Inspect & Identify Issue: Once you schedule a free inspection, we send over one of our wildlife experts to examine the source of the problems that you're experiencing and also examine the seriousness of the issue at hand.
2. Set Traps: We set traps that are specific to the type of wildlife that need is causing you problems.
3. Animal Exclusion: We humanely remove the animal(s) and repair and seal any entry points, like holes or cracks, to mitigate future issues and so that you can rest assured knowing that the wildlife won't be back. We also repair any damage that was done from the animals, such as damaged insulation or drywall.
4. Clean Up & Sanitize: We clean and sanitize the area of all droppings, smell, etc. to the best of our ability.

Wild hogs are dangerous to you and your pets or livestock. They are aggressive and territorial. Wild hogs have sharp teeth and the male hogs have sharp tusks as well. Aside from being aggressive, wild hogs can also carry diseases that can be transmitted to humans.



Do You Have Hogs?

Have you noticed that areas of your lawn or garden are dug up?
Have you noticed that your garbage cans have been tipped over with contents scattered?
Have you noticed hoof prints on your property?
Have you noticed large droppings (3-9 inches long) around your property?
Have you noticed wallows (shallow, muddy depressions) on your property?

Hogs Are Invasive


According to a USDA study, wild hogs can be blamed for $1.5 billion in damages every year in the United States. Hogs consume a large amount of vegetation and they destroy the land they occupy.

  • Feed on crops & plants

  • Invade woodland habitats

  • Destroy golf courses

  • Ruin moist soil land

  • Demolish right of ways

  • Occupy & wreck levees
